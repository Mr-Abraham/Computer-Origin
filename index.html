Abacus

5000 years ago Abacus was developed in china in 3000 B.C. The word abacus means calculating board. It may be consider the first computer used for arithmatic calculations. The abacus is also known as counting frame. The chinese abacus has a frame holding the vertical wires, with seven beads on each wire. A horizontal divider sepatates the top two beads from the bottom five. The arithmatic calculation perfomed by the principle of of positional weight of beads on rack. .

Slide rule

English mathematician E. Gunter dveloped the slide rule. This Machine could perform the operation like additio,subtraction,multiplication, division. Slide rule appeared in the seventeenth century. it consist of two movable rulers placed side by side . Each ruler is marked off in such a way that the distance of the ruler from the beginning are proporstional to the Logarithms of number printed on ruler.

Pascal's Calculator

Blaise pascal was the french mathematician and first scientist to developed and build calculator. He developed a machine that was capable of adding and subtracting the numbers.. The machine was operated by dialing wheel,gears,cylinders.

Leibniz's Multiplication and Dividing machine

Like pascal, Gottfried Leibniz was a seventeenth century scientist who developed the machanical device that could do mathematical calculations .

Difference Engine

Towards the Creation of Computer An English mathematics professor Charles Babbage put the first step. He realized that all mathematical calculations could be broken up into simple operations and they can constantly repeated, and then these operations could be carried out by automatic machine.Charles Babbage started working on "Difference Engine" in 1820s but afrer 10 years he abandoned it for the 'Analytical Engine' -- The real predecessor of Computer

The Analytical Engine

The analytical engine marrks the progression from the arithmetic calculation to the general purpose computations. It was also developed by the "Charles Babbage". This machine was based on the principle that, for certain formulas, the difference between certain value is constant.
The engine had a 'store' (Memory) where the entermediate results could be held, and a separate 'Mill' (Processor) where the arithmetic processing was performed. It had an internal stock of the four arithmatical functions and could perform the direct multiplication and division. It was also capable of functions like looping(Iteration), microprogramming, parallel processing, latching and polling etc.
